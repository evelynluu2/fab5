What is ICS Major Matcher?

Welcome to the UC Irvine School of ICS Major Matcher website! The purpose of this website is to provide students who are interested in majoring within the Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Science information about what each major has to offer and also to help students choose their ideal major.

In order to help students find out which major they would be best suited for we have designed a quiz to analyze their interests on the various topics of information and computer science in order to match them with a major that best addresses those topics. The idea behind this quiz is that if students discover a major that best suits their interests they will be more intrigued with their course work and have a better experience at UCI instead of having to change majors in order to find the right major for themselves. We also provide useful links to the ICS website, counselors, possible career and job opportunities, and others that provide important information to help assist students with their major decision.

The quiz works by asking students a series of questions to get an understanding on what interests them, what doesn’t interest them, and what they believe to be the most exciting topics of ICS. Once students have completed the quiz a major will be suggested to them based on our analysis of their answers and which major covers the topics the student has the most interest in. If the quiz results show no outlying variation among the majors then we may not be able to suggest a major for that student, however we will still provide information to them about each major so that they can research which one would be best for them. Our goal is to give students a head start on finding the right major for them and avoid the need to switch through majors in order to find the one that best suits their interests.

Instruction

Students will be asked two different types of questions in the quiz: One will be multiple choice grid questions which will ask students to rank each answer from 1-5 with 1 being their first choice and 5 being their last choice, and Yes or No binary choice answers which will require the student to answer whether they agree with the proposed question statement or not.

If the student is unsure of an answer or confused by the questions terminology we have provided a glossary page of terms so that can better understand what each question is asking, and also pop-up windows which will give a definition for certain terms.
